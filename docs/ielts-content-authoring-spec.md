# Thinkfy IELTS — Content Authoring Spec

> **Two artifacts:**
> - **This spec** — the standard: workflow, conventions, per-skill authoring guide, band rubrics + scoring math (the source of truth for both human authors and the AI scorer in WS-3.1/3.2), and how it maps to the engine schema.
> - **The fillable workbook** — `docs/ielts-content-authoring-template.xlsx` (10 tabs; what teachers actually type into; one row per item; import-ready for WS-1.1).
>
> Goal: your teachers start authoring the seed bank **today**, in parallel with all coding work. Content (hand-authored) is the launch long-pole; this unblocks it now.

---

## 1. Workflow & roles

`Draft → In QA → Approved → Published` (track in the workbook's **Status** column; `Needs fix` bounces an item back). Always fill **Author**, and **QA Reviewer** after review. A second teacher QAs (correctness, answer key, difficulty, register) before Approved.

## 2. Content quality bar (non-negotiable)

1. **Original content only.** Do **not** paste passages/audio/questions from Cambridge or other copyrighted tests. Author original items modeled on the official formats. Note any public-domain/own-source adaptation in the Originality/Notes cell. (This is also why we can hand-author safely — no licensing exposure.)
2. **Unambiguous answers.** Every objective item has exactly one defensible correct answer + **accept-variants** (spelling/synonym/number forms) where relevant.
3. **Bilingual explanations.** Every item gets an explanation in **both English and Vietnamese** (VN-first learners).
4. **Honest difficulty.** Easy / Medium / Hard, calibrated — drives adaptive practice + seeding.
5. **Writing & Speaking ship with a Band-9 model** answer/notes **and** per-criterion examiner notes (TA/TR, CC, LR, GRA for Writing; FC, LR, GRA, Pron for Speaking).

## 3. Seed-bank target for launch (lean; grow weekly after)

| Skill | Launch target |
|---|---|
| Reading | 2–3 full sets (3 passages / ~40 Qs each) + per-type drills |
| Listening | 2–3 full sets (4 sections / ~40 Qs each) — **scripts only**; audio is TTS-generated (WS-1.3) |
| Writing | 8–12 Task 2 prompts + 6–8 Task 1 prompts, each with a Band-9 model |
| Speaking | 6–8 full Part 1/2/3 sets |

## 4. Per-skill authoring guide

**Reading** (Academic): 3 passages, ~40 questions, 60 min. Passages ≈700–900 words, academic register, 4–6 paragraphs. Author the passage (Reading Passages tab) then its questions (Reading Questions tab) referencing the Passage ID. Mix question types per passage. Question types: `true_false_notgiven`, `yes_no_notgiven`, `matching_headings`, `matching_information`, `matching_features`, `sentence_completion`, `summary_completion`, `note_table_form_flowchart_completion`, `short_answer`, `diagram_label`, `mcq_single`, `mcq_multi`. Always state the word limit in Instructions ("NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS").

**Listening:** 4 sections, ~40 questions, 30 min (+10 transfer). Section 1 = everyday social (2 speakers); 2 = social monologue; 3 = academic (up to 4 speakers); 4 = academic monologue/lecture. Author the **script** (Listening Scripts tab) with speaker accents (UK/US/AUS) — audio is generated by TTS later. Embed answers naturally with realistic distractors/corrections. Types: completion types, `mcq_*`, `matching_features`, `map_plan_label`, `short_answer`.

**Writing:** Task 1 Academic (describe a visual, 150+ words, 20 min) — attach/describe the chart/graph/process/map in the Visual/Data cell. Task 1 General (a letter). Task 2 (argument/discussion essay, 250+ words, 40 min). Each prompt ships with a Band-9 model + per-criterion notes.

**Speaking:** Part 1 (interview Q&A), Part 2 (cue card: prompt + 4 bullets, 1 min prep / 1–2 min talk), Part 3 (two-way discussion tied to the Part 2 topic). Ship sample Band-9 answers/notes + per-criterion notes.

The **Question-Type Reference** tab lists every type's engine key + expected fields.

## 5. Band rubrics (source of truth for the AI scorer)

Summarized from the official **public** band descriptors — cross-check against the official **2024** descriptors before launch (a credibility cue VN learners now expect). Each criterion weighted **25%**. Full per-band tables are in the workbook's **Rubrics (reference)** tab; condensed here for the WS-3.1/3.2 scorer prompts.

### Writing — 4 criteria
- **Task Response (T2) / Task Achievement (T1)** — does it fully address all parts of the task, with a clear position (T2) or a clear overview + accurate key features (T1), and relevant, extended, supported ideas?
- **Coherence & Cohesion** — logical organization, progression, paragraphing, and natural (not mechanical/over-used) cohesive devices.
- **Lexical Resource** — range, precision, collocation, less-common vocabulary; spelling/word-formation.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy** — range of structures (simple + complex), error frequency, punctuation.

### Speaking — 4 criteria
- **Fluency & Coherence** — pace/continuity, hesitation (content- vs language-related), logical connection.
- **Lexical Resource** — range, paraphrase, idiomatic/topic-appropriate use.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy** — variety of structures + accuracy.
- **Pronunciation** — sounds, word stress, intonation, intelligibility (officially detailed at bands 2/4/6/8; even bands interpolate). This criterion is augmented by the **phoneme-level** analysis (Azure Speech, WS-3.3).

> The scorer must output **per-criterion sub-scores 0–9** + the computed band + the math — never a single black-box number. Band anchors (5/6/7/8/9) per criterion are in the workbook table.

## 6. Scoring math (encode exactly)

- **Writing:** each criterion 0–9 → task band = mean of the 4. **Overall Writing = (Task 1 × 1/3) + (Task 2 × 2/3)**, rounded to nearest 0.5. *(Task 2 counts double.)*
- **Speaking:** Speaking band = mean of the 4 criteria, rounded to nearest 0.5.
- **Reading / Listening:** correct out of 40 → band via the conversion table (§7).
- **Overall test band** = mean of the 4 skill bands, rounded to nearest half band; **.25 rounds up to .5, .75 rounds up to the next whole** (6.25→6.5, 6.75→7.0).

## 8. Mapping to the engine

The workbook columns map 1:1 to the IELTS schema (masterplan §6) and are **import-ready for WS-1.1** — keep headers unchanged. Specifically:
- Reading Passages / Listening Scripts → `passages` / `listening_sections` (+ `audio_assets` after TTS).
- Reading/Listening Questions → typed question rows (the `Question Type (key)` = the engine enum); auto-graded by WS-1.2 renderers/validators (accept-variants honored).
- Writing/Speaking Prompts → prompt rows; the **Band-9 model** seeds the exemplar RAG corpus used by the Writing scorer (WS-3.1); the per-criterion notes inform the scorer rubric bundles.
- The **Rubrics** + **Scoring math** here are the canonical spec the WS-3.1/3.2 prompt bundles implement.

This spec + workbook is also the basis for the future Duolingo/Brilliant-style **Learn-mode** bite-size activities (masterplan §5) — the same item bank, delivered as micro-lessons rather than only as timed mocks.
